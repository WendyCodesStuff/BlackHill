Short Single Page

A short single page website takes up one screen. It doesn't scroll and doesn't link to other pages within the site, although it can link to external sites. This site is the simplest to produce and takes up very little space on the server. We use short single page sites for campaigns or one-off events eg, a book release, a music festival. See Beach Hut 24 for an example of this kind of site.

In this example the title Beach Hut 24 is splashed across the centre, with a Book button below which links to AirBnB. An elegant responsive menu in the top right shows as a simple navigation bar for larger screens and a drop-down hamburger icon for phones.






Multi-Panelled or Long Page





By selecting several panels, we can design a long page, where a customer can scroll down and see all your company has to offer on the one page. Our own front page is an example of a long page (although ours does link to other internal pages, which a multi-panelled single-page wouldn't). See the long image for an example of how this would look if you could see the whole page at once.

Types of panel to choose from include:

  • Full screen image to head your page - can include navigation bar and/or branding on the top or centre of screen-wide image.


  • Pure copy - giving you the space to inform customers of your services, campaigns and whatever information you need to share.


  • Grid-style cards such as our display of recent projects. In this case each card has a title, image and tagline and the card links to the relevant page. There are many uses and layout options for cards, such as "Meet the Team" with a picture and short bio of your staff.


  • Testimonial quotes giving a splash of credibility to your service.


  • Tiles - which are similar to cards but are images without text and give a stylish way to showcase your work.


  • Meet the Team - headshot and short bio of key team members add tangible credibility to your site.


  • Footer bar - a great place for social links and contact information.


If you have any requirements or ideas for other types of panel then of course we can discuss this. We are always researching new ways to present your data always with a creative eye on useability.

Take a look at some of our other projects for examples of long pages in action.


Multi Page Website

Even using a multi-panelled page does have its limitations. When you have a large amount of content or variety that doesn't fit well on the same page, then of course multiple pages can be deployed.


Our own webpage is an example of this kind of site and although it takes longer to design and develop; and inevitably costs more in terms of development and deployment, it is often the preferred choice for many companies.
